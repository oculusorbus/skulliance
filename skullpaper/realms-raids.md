# Raids

## Overview

Realms participants can engage in raids and monitor the status of outgoing/incoming raids.

## Offense & Defense Ratings

Every raid is decided by two ratings, each averaged from four inputs (rounded up):

* **Offense** = `ceil((Armory + Barracks + Crypt + BarracksScore) ÷ 4)`
* **Defense** = `ceil((Tower + Factory + Mine + TowerScore) ÷ 4)`

**BarracksScore** rises as your deployed, trained army fills your capacity, and **TowerScore** rises with your Tower garrison (up to 10 soldiers). This is why location levels alone don't win raids - you also need a trained, deployed, and garrisoned army. See [[realms-locations]] and [[realms-soldiers]].

Your **success rate** is derived from the gap between your offense and the defender's defense. Two evenly matched Realms each sit near 50%; a stronger attacker's rate climbs from there. Stocked defensive consumables can nudge the defender's odds up, and stocked offensive consumables nudge the attacker's odds up (each side capped at +10%).

## Raid Offense

Successful raids initiated by Realm owners result in damaging a random defense location for the defender's Realm. A percentage of a random project's points, containing a balance of at least 100, are also looted from the defender's balance and redistributed to the attacker. That percentage is based on the difference between the attacker's offense and the player's defense.

If the attacking realm is stronger than the defending realm, range restrictions limit the max percentage that can be looted to 3% of the random project points depending on the difference between offense and defense levels.

If the attacking realm is weaker than the defending realm, the max percentage looted can be up to 9% depending on the difference between offense and defense levels. This ensures that weaker realms are rewarded in points according to the level of risk they undertook by raiding a stronger realm.

### Offensive Strategy

Attackers should seek out realms with the highest success rates for raids. This increases the likelihood that the raid will be successful and that the attacker will be successful in looting points. If attackers are mainly interested in looting more project points, filtering realms by wealth and attacking wealthy realms may yield a higher payout. Especially if the Realm is more than 3 levels stronger and the weaker attacker is willing to take more risk to try and hit a higher percentage points jackpot.

## Raid Defense

If the attacker fails during the raid, a random location from their offense is damaged by 1 level and the defender's same offense location is upgraded by 1 level - and this defensive upgrade is **not** capped at Level 10, so strong defenders push their locations past the purchasable soft limit (see [[realms-locations]]). There is also a 1 in 3 chance that the attacker's portal will be damaged by 1 level, reducing the attacker's number of concurrent raids allotted.

### Defensive Strategy

Claiming daily rewards increases the amount of project balances for the defender. By having multiple balances, there is less likelihood that an attacker will loot their highest points balance. Acquiring at least 1 NFT from a new project and staking it helps to also diversify points balances and insulate from attackers looting a high value points balance.

## Raid Stats

Raid stats highlight both monthly and all time statistics regarding raid performance. It also lists the total number of raids, number of raids in progress, successful raids, raid failures, and a calculated score of raid performance. It also contains links to the monthly and all time raids leaderboards.

## Outgoing Raids

Outgoing Raids are attacks that you've initiated against other Realms.

### Outgoing Pending

This section highlights raids you've initiated that are currently in progress.

### Outgoing Completed

## Incoming Raids

Incoming Raids are attacks initiated by other Realms against you.

### Incoming Pending

This section highlights other realms currently in the process of raiding your realm.

### Incoming Completed

This section highlights the results of recent incoming raids on your realm.

## Monthly Leaderboard

The monthly leaderboard for Raids showcases the performance of participants. All raid participants earn their fair share of 1 million CARBON based on their performance. CARBON is crucial for upgrading Realm locations because it can be burned to form DIAMOND and then shattered to equal parts core project points through Crafting. The Crafting panel can be found in the sidebar of the Dashboard or Store pages. Raid participants should garner a substantial amount of CARBON every month preventing them from having to rely on the 3x alternative point cost to upgrade locations.
